Dalil & Hadits

Why Seek Knowledge

A few hadith that remind us why the walk to a majelis is never a small thing.

HR. Thabrani — The Great Reward
مَنْ غَدَا إِلَى الْمَسْجِدِ لَا يُرِيدُ إِلَّا أَنْ يَتَعَلَّمَ خَيْرًا أَوْ يُعَلِّمَهُ كَانَ لَهُ كَأَجْرِ حَاجٍّ تَامًّا حَجَّتُهُ

Man ghada ilal masjidi la yuridu illa an yata'allama khayran aw yu'allimahu kana lahu ka'ajri hajjin tamman hajjatuhu

"Whoever goes to the mosque to learn something good or to teach it — they will receive a reward equal to that of a complete Hajj."

"Barangsiapa yang berangkat ke masjid dengan tujuan mempelajari atau mengajarkan kebaikan, maka baginya pahala seperti orang yang menunaikan haji secara sempurna."

HR. Thabrani — Shahih Al-Targhib no. 86
HR. Muslim — The Path to Paradise
مَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَلْتَمِسُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا سَهَّلَ اللهُ لَهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ

Man salaka thariqan yaltamisu fihi 'ilman sahhalallahu lahu bihi thariqan ilal jannah

"Whoever takes a path in search of knowledge, Allah will make easy for them a path to Paradise."

"Barangsiapa menempuh suatu jalan untuk mencari ilmu, Allah akan mudahkan baginya jalan menuju surga."

HR. Muslim no. 2699
HR. Muslim — Gathering to Learn
وَمَا اجْتَمَعَ قَوْمٌ فِي بَيْتٍ مِنْ بُيُوتِ اللهِ يَتْلُونَ كِتَابَ اللهِ وَيَتَدَارَسُونَهُ بَيْنَهُمْ إِلَّا نَزَلَتْ عَلَيْهِمُ السَّكِينَةُ

Wama ijtama'a qawmun fi baytin min buyutillahi yatluna kitaballahi wa yatadarasunahu baynahum illa nazalat 'alayhimus sakinatu...

"No group gathers in one of the houses of Allah, reciting and studying the Qur'an together, except that tranquility descends upon them, mercy envelops them, angels surround them, and Allah mentions them to those in His presence."

"Tidaklah suatu kaum berkumpul di salah satu rumah Allah membaca Al-Qur'an dan mempelajarinya bersama, kecuali turunlah ketenangan atas mereka, rahmat meliputi mereka, para malaikat mengelilingi mereka, dan Allah menyebut-nyebut mereka di hadapan para malaikat-Nya."

HR. Muslim no. 2699
HR. Abu Dawud — Angels Lower Their Wings
إِنَّ الْمَلَائِكَةَ لَتَضَعُ أَجْنِحَتَهَا رِضًا لِطَالِبِ الْعِلْمِ

Innal mala'ikata lata dha'u ajnihatahar ridhan lithalibil 'ilm

"Indeed the angels lower their wings in approval for the one who seeks knowledge."

"Sesungguhnya para malaikat benar-benar merendahkan sayap-sayapnya karena ridha kepada penuntut ilmu."

HR. Abu Dawud no. 3641 — Shahih
HR. Ibn Majah — An Obligation
طَلَبُ الْعِلْمِ فَرِيضَةٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُسْلِمٍ

Thalabul 'ilmi faridhatun 'ala kulli muslim

"Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."

"Menuntut ilmu adalah kewajiban bagi setiap muslim."

HR. Ibnu Majah no. 224 — Shahih
Atsar Ibn Abbas — Tie Knowledge
قَيِّدُوا الْعِلْمَ بِالْكِتَابَةِ

Qayyidul 'ilma bil kitabah

"Tie knowledge by writing it down." — This is why bringing a notebook to a majelis has always been encouraged by the scholars.

"Ikatlah ilmu dengan tulisan." — Inilah mengapa para ulama selalu menganjurkan membawa catatan ke setiap majelis.

Atsar Ibnu Abbas — Shahih
Etiquette for Attending a Majelis
01
Start with Salam

Say Assalamu'alaikum when you enter. It sets the tone — bringing peace into the space and grounding your intention. Send salawat upon the Prophet ﷺ. If possible, pray Tahiyyatul Masjid before sitting.

02
Check Your Intention

Knowledge hits differently when it's for Allah. Pause for a second and remind yourself: "I'm here to seek His pleasure."

03
Sit with Humility

You're a student — just like everyone else in the room. Listen fully. Don't interrupt. Knowledge settles best in a calm heart.

04
Take Notes

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Tie knowledge by writing it down." One small note today can guide you for a lifetime.

05
Put Your Phone Away

Notifications can wait. Be fully present — not just physically, but mentally too. That's part of respecting the knowledge and the one teaching it.

06
Make Du'a for the Scholars

Ustadz and ulama carry a heavy responsibility. Make du'a for them, their families, and all the Muslims — asking Allah for forgiveness for the entire ummah. A sincere du'a might be unseen, but it matters more than you think.

07
Seek Knowledge with Purpose

Come with the intention to learn, ask Allah for beneficial knowledge, and aim to actually live what you learn.

Du'a Before Learning
سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا وَعَمَلًا مُتَقَبَّلًا

Subhanaka Allahumma inni as'aluka 'ilman nafi'an wa 'amalan mutaqabbalan

Ya Allah, aku memohon kepada-Mu ilmu yang bermanfaat dan amal yang diterima.

Du'a to Close the Session
سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ أَسْتَغْفِرُكَ وَأَتُوبُ إِلَيْكَ

Subhanaka Allahumma wa bihamdika, asyhadu an la ilaha illa anta, astaghfiruka wa atubu ilaik

Maha suci Engkau ya Allah dan dengan memuji-Mu. Aku bersaksi tiada ilah selain Engkau. Aku memohon ampun dan bertaubat kepada-Mu.

Common Questions

FAQ & Tips

Things people usually wonder about before attending their first kajian.

How do I check kajian schedules?
The easiest way is to follow each mosque's official Instagram. In the Mosque Directory, every card has a "Kajian Schedule" button that links directly to their page.

Most mosques post schedules weekly or a day before the session. Check their Highlights or recent posts for the latest.
Are these kajian open to the public?
Most kajian listed here are open and free for everyone. However, some may be limited to certain groups or require prior registration.

It's always best to confirm via Instagram or mosque contact — especially if it's your first time attending.
What should I bring?
A few things worth bringing:

• Notebook & pen — to capture beneficial knowledge. "Tie knowledge by writing it down."
• Qur'an or kitab — especially for a kitab-based study circle
• Proper, modest attire — Allah loves beauty, and coming to the masjid in your best state is part of adab
• Wudhu (if possible) — come in a state of cleanliness
• Open mindset — come to learn, not to judge
Is it okay to arrive late?
It's okay — honestly, better than not coming at all. Just keep the adab:

• Enter quietly without distracting others
• Sit in the nearest available spot
• Avoid stepping over people
• Save your questions for after the session
Can I record the kajian?
It depends on the mosque and the ustadz. Always ask for permission first.

Some allow personal recordings but not redistribution without consent. Be mindful of other attendees' privacy too.

Many mosques already upload recordings to YouTube or official channels — check their Instagram first.
Can I bring children?
It depends on the mosque — some have dedicated areas for parents with children or more family-friendly spaces. Before coming, check their Instagram or contact the admin.

If you do bring a child: prepare quiet activities (books, small toys), and sit near the exit for flexibility. Being considerate helps keep the space comfortable for everyone.
What if a mosque isn't listed here?
This page is community-driven and continuously growing. If you know a mosque in Jabodetabek that actively holds sunnah kajian and isn't listed here — feel free to reach out and suggest it.
